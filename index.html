From me to you.

It's a nice day isn't it? A new day with fresh breeze under the same sun or moon.
This gives you a lot of ideas to do or to make. Sometimes, you can use this opportunity to relax and unwind, simply because, it's just a very nice and slow day.

I was wondering, did you ever felt like you've been doing something you love and then in all the sudden reasons, you felt like you were stuck? Like a jammed sewing machine, or a clugged sink, you know you need to keep moving forward, yet somehow, your mind doesn't listen to you anymore.

I found it really funny. I thought it was just some emotional unstableness or a sudden retribution of fatigue that could be dealt or managed through sleeping or relaxing, but as you continue to ignore it, you just one day find yourself nose-dived into a pit full of darkness.

My music stopped responding to me. I felt like it wants to evolve, yet I ignored it and pushed it to some areas that I should be inclined to do.

Growing up, I was exposed to a music that is rarely accepted to the music scene in the modern days.

Even so, I accepted the fact and tough it out in my junior, secondary and college education. Looking for bands who will be interested with the music I am capable with, but none of them were interested.

I thought it would be great to have a band that would love the same music and appreciate it in the same way that I do, but then again, I am still alone in this music journey.

When I made Archard Deity, it was for the purpose of music production experimentation, to see if I am capable of making songs on my own since I was eager to release the songs that I enjoy.

But don't get me wrong, all the songs that I made, I enjoyed making them, writing, arranging, mixing, mastering and producing. I even got a chance to collaborate with people who has amazing talents on what they do. I am just blown-away and honored.

I also got the chance to have a unit Re:Play, and a rock band Rocket Rock Boom. I won't stop making songs with them, since I enjoyed their company and would love to continuously progress with them as long as they allow me to be their Producer.

But for the meantime, I can't write songs until I managed to bring back that sound that my music gives to me. I'll do whatever it takes just to get my sound back.

I'll be practicing on my own again, until then.
